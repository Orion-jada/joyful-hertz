   1. EXAMPLES FROM PAST YEARS:   
      1. Femininizine- Women In Stories: Our mission is to educate others on feminism and feminist ideals through written, creative, and visual art. 

      

      2. The Memory Monthly: The Memory Monthly was created as a way to convey the importance of memory and how it affects us. The way we perceive the past, act in the present, and foretell the future is determined by how we remember specific moments and their corresponding emotions. Memories of the past shape our personal and shared identities and play a larger role in the acquisition of knowledge than is generally realized. The Memory Magazine's purpose is to allow others to discover the role of memories in their life through featured stories and pieces of literature.

      

      3. Isolation Magazine: Dear Readers, The feeling of isolation is something we all have felt at one time or another, especially now coming out of the pandemic. That shows that even if we feel like we are alone, we aren’t. This magazine is here to show that there is always someone who can relate. There is always someone who knows your pain. These pieces of literature hopefully connect with the reader and make them feel understood and comforted. Our goal, as the editors, is to provide pieces of literature, both original and from various other authors, that connect with the readers and model different versions and sides of isolation. We hope that you enjoy what we put together\!  
           
      4. *Concluant* is a magazine that focuses on endings \- in stories and in life. In April 2020, Aakash Jha established *The Concluant* during quarantine, which he believed marked the end of his social life, at least for the foreseeable future. Now, *The Concluant* is the most widely-read annual magazine dedicated to documenting all things ending. From movies, to graduations, to funerals, we cover them all. Our goal is to provide insight on everything that has an ending, from literature to the very concept of conclusion and closure. We live to satisfy all those who are reminiscent of the things long gone. "Don't cry because it's over. Smile because it happened." \-Dr. Seuss

## **Literary Magazine Final Project Rubric**

### **Section 1: Individual Accountability (50%)**

*These points are awarded to each student based on your specific contributions.*

| Category | Mastery (5 pts) | Proficient (3 pts) | Emerging (1-2 pts) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Original Contribution** | One unique, high-quality piece (e.g., story, song, art). Not a category used by a teammate. | One unique piece included. Fits the theme and meets length/effort requirements. | Piece is missing, incomplete, or a duplicate category of a teammate. |
| **Curated Selection** | Included one outside work that fits the theme perfectly with a clear citation and description of how it connects to the theme.  | Included one outside work from another author that connects to the theme. | Outside work is missing or has no clear connection to the magazine's theme. |
| **Quality of Work** | Individual pieces are polished, proofread, and show high creative effort. | Work is complete and clear, with only minor errors in writing or design. | Work appears rushed, unfinished, or lacks professional effort. |

### 

### **Section 2: Group Production & Design (50%)**

*These points are awarded to the entire team for the final product.*

| Category | Mastery (5 pts) | Proficient (3 pts) | Emerging (1-2 pts) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Mission & Editorial** | Letter from the Editors is professional, signed by all, and sets a clear, engaging tone. | Letter from the Editors states the mission and highlights content. | Letter is missing or does not explain the purpose/theme of the magazine. |
| **Required Components** | All components (Cover, TOC, Back Page, Staff Page, Ads) are present and creatively designed. | All components are present and functional. | Missing one or more core components (e.g., no TOC or only 1 advertisement). |
| **Menu Items (3)** | Three menu items are highly creative, interactive, and deeply thematic. | Three menu items (Quiz, Playlist, etc.) are included and relate to the theme. | Fewer than three menu items included, or items are unrelated to the theme. |
| **Two Pages with Connections to Literature and Kafka** | Two pieces of writing are included that connect to a work we’ve read this year and a Kafka work or the author himself. The selections connect to the theme of the magazine and are thoughtfully created. It’s apparent it was a group effort.  | Two pieces of writing are present and connections are there, but they don’t fully align to the theme of the magazine. Not as thoughtfully done.  | One piece of writing only or the component is missing entirely.  |
| **Theme & Aesthetics** | Magazine has a cohesive, professional look. The theme "Stories" is interpreted uniquely. | Magazine is attractive and easy to read. Theme is consistent throughout. | Layout is messy or disjointed. It is difficult to see how the content connects. |
