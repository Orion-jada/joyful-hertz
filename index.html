An Accelerated History of AI

by Aaryan Gada

01 October 2022

As the final months of 2022 bleed into a bitter and uncertain winter, the global order threatens to fracture under the weight of tectonic geopolitical rifts and a relentless economic storm.

01
02 October 2022

The shadow of the war in Ukraine lengthens across Europe, while the structural scars of a post-pandemic era refuse to heal, leaving the international community teetering on the edge of an unpredictable abyss.

02
03 November 2022

Beneath this looming dread, sovereign states scramble to fortify their domestic borders against a cascade of crises, as runaway inflation erodes stability and soaring energy costs cast a chilling pall over households and industries alike.

03
04 November 2022

In Europe, deep internal divisions threaten to tear the European Union apart over a desperate bid to cap natural gas prices, and in Cambodia, defense chiefs from the U.S. and China prepare to meet under a fragile truce, their tense dialogue serving as a fleeting buffer against a gathering global storm.

04
05 November 2022

In Washington, the United States escalates its economic warfare by stripping Russia of its “market economy” status—a sharp pivot that underscores America's aggressive efforts to insulate its own domestic landscape.

05
06 Mid-November 2022

Yet, even as the Federal Reserve continues its relentless tightening cycle, the U.S. economy offers only a deceptive, highly volatile reprieve. The battered American stock market claws its way back from deep autumn lows, but this recovery is a frantic, hyper-anxious display.

06
07 Mid-November 2022

Investors stretch themselves to the absolute limit, desperately clinging to tentative signs of cooling inflation, swelling a fragile bubble of strained optimism.

07
08 Late November 2022

It is an atmosphere wound so incredibly tight that the entire global apparatus seems to hold its breath, waiting in the silence for the single, sharp vibration that will shatter the illusion of control.

08
09 November 22, 2022

And then, on November 22nd, OpenAI releases ChatGPT.

GPT
ChatGPT - November 22, 2022 Release
GPT
Hello. I am a language model trained by OpenAI. Ask me anything about the global landscape of late 2022, or the storm that has been brewing.
ME
Explain the conditions of your release.
GPT
My release, what one could call the Netscape moment of artificial intelligence, marks the first time that the general public gains access to sophisticated large language models. My release took the internet by storm, shattering all previous records of user acquisition in digital history, hitting the 1 and 100 million user mark in 5 days and 2 months respectively. The model behind me is GPT-3.5, was a finetuned version of the 175 billion parameter model GPT-3, a breakthrough in natural language processing.
10 Late 2022

The Initial Spark

November 2022: OpenAI releases ChatGPT. Powered by a fine-tuned version of GPT-3.5, specifically GPT-3.5 turbo, it marks the first time that the broad public has access to frontier large language models. This introduction of generative artificial intelligence changes the human-machine interaction paradigm, kicking off a hyper-accelerated AI arms race in Silicon Valley. This event is the “Netscape moment” of the artificial intelligence industry.

2022
11 2023

The Great Scaling and Multimodal Wars

Early 2023: Big tech scrambles to respond. Microsoft launches a major partnership with OpenAI, integrating GPT models throughout the Office suite of applications. Google deploys its experimental conversational model, Bard. Early adoption of ChatGPT makes it a sensation around the internet.

March 2023: OpenAI releases GPT-4, a model characterized by being a massive leap in reasoning, problem-solving, and standardized test performance.

July 2023: Meta enters the AI race with Llama 2, open-sourcing its weights and igniting a global developer movement to build, fine-tune, and run local models, removing the reliance on proprietary APIs.

2023
12 2024

Multimodal Breakthroughs & The Dawn of Agency

Early to Mid 2024: AI transitions from pure text interfaces such as the common chatbot to multimodal systems. Google introduces the Gemini 1.5 Pro model, boasting a groundbreaking context window of 2 million tokens. OpenAI releases GPT-4o, the “o” representing “omni.” The model introduced voice, text, and image processing natively within the model at high speeds.

Late 2024: The industry shifts focus from "stateless" chat interfaces to "stateful" agentic workflows. OpenAI introduces its o-series reasoning models, demonstrating "System 2" reflective thinking, allowing models to deliberate and self-correct before responding.

2024
13 2025

Market Disruptions & The Agentic Inflection

January 2025: The geopolitical landscape of AI shifts dramatically as Chinese startup DeepSeek releases highly efficient, low-cost open-weights models. The sudden release of frontier-class capabilities in an open-weight model triggers a historic tech-stock sell-off, erasing hundreds of billions of dollars in market cap from hardware giants. This event was known as the “Deepseek moment.”

Mid-to-Late 2025: Agentic AI moves into active production. Anthropic previews the groundbreaking Claude Code, a terminal-based agentic coding tool, transforming the AI from an assistant into an autonomous teammate. OpenAI launches GPT-5, pushing the boundary of complex, multi-step problem solving.

2025
14 2026

The Agentic Frontier

Present Day: Chatbots are largely a relic of the past, or more accurately, only used by people who are not on the frontier of AI. The industry is defined by autonomous agentic systems capable of executing extended, hours-long tasks, from complex software engineering and continuous scientific research to managing entire business pipelines with minimal human intervention.
